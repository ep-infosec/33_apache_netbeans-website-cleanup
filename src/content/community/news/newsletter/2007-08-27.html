Introducing the New NetBeans.tv!

In an effort to better support our growing number of community videos, NetBeans.tv has undergone a major face lift and now includes additional functionality.

To celebrate the launch of NetBeans.tv, we hope that you will visit and join the community section of the site. Doing this also means a chance to win prizes! Include your video or photo, tell us how you use NetBeans and why James Gosling should visit you in your hometown.

NBTV is just getting started, so if you have content for the other sections of the site contact us using the provided form. Help us grow!

Make NetBeans 6.0 Rock - Join NetCAT 6.0!

Do you want to help shape NetBeans? Then join NetCAT! NetCAT (NetBeans Community Acceptance Testing program) is a team of select community members who provide feedback on product usability, quality and performance of the NetBeans IDE.

NetBeans 6.0 is entering its stabilization phase and we need your help with testing. If you have experience with the NetBeans IDE and want to help make 6.0 the best IDE around, then join NetCAT! All we ask for is your time and effort. But hurry! The deadline for applications is Wednesday, August 29th.

A Creative Use of the NetBeans Visual Library

Dream Team-er Fabrizio Giudici, gave NetBeans's Visual Library a try after watching one of Roman Strobl's screencast. Giudici's assessment? "The Visual Library is one of the coolest things that the NetBeans guys delivered with NetBeans 6. It is a rich API which allows you to create a sort of "blackboard" where objects can be added, removed, edited, moved, resized, and connected in a visual graph." Read his blog entry for more on how he used the Visual Library to implement a new feature in his open source digital photo management system, blueMarine.
